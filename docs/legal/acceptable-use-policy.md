## You may not use Aio to

- Break the law, or help anyone else break the law.
- Generate or facilitate malware, attacks against systems you don't own or
  have authorization to test, or other harmful security exploitation.
- Harass, defraud, impersonate, or deceive others.
- Generate child sexual abuse material, or any content sexualizing minors.
- Attempt to bypass rate limits, spend caps, or invite-cohort gates
  (`AIO_BETA_INVITE_ONLY`, `AIO_BETA_SPEND_CAP_CREDITS`) introduced for the
  beta period.
- Extract, scrape, or resell another user's data accessed through a shared
  or compromised account.
- Send excessive automated load intended to degrade the service for other
  users.

## Tooling and connected accounts

Saved Agents, scheduled tasks, and connected credentials run with your
account's permissions. You're responsible for the actions any tool call,
schedule, or saved agent you configure actually takes — including against
third-party services you connect.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in a warning, rate-limit reduction, suspension, or
account termination, proportionate to severity. Suspected abuse is logged
to `aio_audit_log` and reviewed per
`docs/operations/support-intake.md`'s triage flow ("Abuse / trust" class).
Systemic or severe abuse may be escalated per
`docs/operations/incident-response.md`.
